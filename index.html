Insight: The false morality of hate

To combat hate and violence, we must understand the stories hateful people tell themselves to justify their actions.

Moral foundations theory is a theory of how humans rationalize moral decisions. The theory states that all humans ground their moral reasoning in six "moral foundations". However, only some of these moral foundations lead to hate and violence.

By carefully understanding our moral psychology we can build a global civilization that is largely immune to political hate and violence.

Insight: Conspiracy Theories

Where do conspiracy beliefs come from? Recent behavioral research suggests that they do not reflect pathology or lazy thinking but may instead come from normal minds that feel chronically threatened.

Conspiracy theories are everywhere. They lead otherwise rational people to believe false narratives. While irrational, the tendency to believe conspiracies is embedded in our evolutionary history as prey-animals and the psychological intersection of belief and threat.

By understanding the psychology of conspiracy theories, we can combat them in our own psyche and work to exercise them from the body politic.

Principle: Secularism

Secularism is the best way to protect freedom of religion on a spiritually pluralistic planet. Secularism works both ways - while it does not permit religion to interfere in affairs of government, it also forbids the state from seeking to control religion. This is the best way to ensure that those with faith can pursue their beliefs unencumbered.

Secularism provides a safe container for the free exchange of religious ideas, accerating the evolution of human spirituality.

Anti Fascist: Embracing Complexity

We live in the most complicated society to ever exist. Fascist ideologies grow when people feel overwhelmed by the complexities of life and grope for simplistic explanations for the chaos they see around them. These "epistimalogical cartoons" seek simplicty, blame and comfort in the midst of bewildering complexity.

By encouraging people to stay epistimalogically grounded in the midst of the dizzying complexity of modern society, we can move forward with a politics of hope that embraces the rapid pace of change we see around us.

Science: Evidence based policy

Evidence-based policy is public policy informed by rigorously established scientific evidence.

Evidence-based policy uses scientific evidence about what works. In the field of medicine, public policies based on scientifically-rigorous evidence have produced extraordinary advances in health over the past 50 years. By contrast, in most areas of social policy – such as education, poverty reduction, and crime prevention – government programs often are implemented with little regard to evidence, costing billions of dollars yet failing to address critical social problems.
